Collaborative prototyping

My role

I Worked as Product Designer in the National Research Infrastructure platform, an initiative from the Ministry of Science and Technology. when I was a UX analyst consultant at Vertigo for The National Education and Research Network.

About the platform

Image: Homepage of the National Research Infrastructure Platform

The platform gathers information about laboratories and equipment of science and research institutions, enabling researchers to find equipment and facilities across the country and request the use of these resources among different institutions and locations across the country to foster innovation.

Moreover the Ministry also aims to obtain information and data to promote public policies for the maintenance of existing infrastructures and acquisition of new ones.

We integrated developer reviews into the discovery phase and switched to using Figma for prototyping. This change promoted collaboration and empowered all parties involved. The client and user validation process became more technical and efficient, and developers were better able to understand the prototype and the user journeys. This process was later refined and implemented in other projects, and Figma became the official prototyping tool for the company.

Discovery process

The discovery process was composed by a group of diverse users from research institutions who participated in weekly conversations with the product team and the Ministry of Science and Technology (the client).

Image: Product creation around the prototype

we organized rounds for the weekly conversations for each new user journey (e.g. login, advanced search...). The first sessions consisted on discussions to gather requirements of user needs and realities based on the platform business goals and the product current reality.

With these requirements, the product manager and I as product designer worked on concept prototypes conducting regular feedback sessions to better include stakeholders in the creative process:

- With the client and users, we collaborated using the prototypes at figma. This process not only helped us to validate the solutions faster, but also helped them the impact of new features in the business and marketing strategies.

- We were looking to provide the developing team more agency in the creative process before was turned into their implementation backlog in addition to give them time to propose proof of concepts and discuss the implementation challenges on a earlier stage.

Image:Fragment of user journey: How users navigate inside and outside the platform to register an institution

Design and development

After the ideas were validated, the prototypes was specified and added to the development backlog. My main responsibilities included defining the user scenarios and interface with the product owner, evolving the design system as necessary, and ensuring the project's assets and content were provided (such as marketing banners and text content).

Image: Example of design delivery - interface specs for production

Product launch and evaluation

After the MVP was launched, we monitored the experience through Google Analytics and users' feedback coming from the support team. We planned and prioritized a new roadmap for the product evolution for the existing features and the strategy to include the new features focused on a new user profile, researchers looking for information on science and research Infrastructures and their sharing possibilities.
