How AI Changes the Way We Interact with Products

As Quorum, an enterprise software for US government affairs, started experimenting with AI, our product team faced a key challenge: how to move beyond a feature-driven platform while unlocking AI's potential in ways that truly served user needs.
2023 6 months Design Manager (Quorum)

My Role

As AI became a strategic pillar at Quorum, I partnered closely with the AI team and its product designer in my role as Product Design Manager, supporting strategy through research and design facilitation.

I began by leading UX research, analyzing prompts and studying patterns to uncover critical insights that shaped how users first perceived AI within the product. From there, I collaborated on evaluating our initial release, pinpointing gaps and opportunities to refine the user experience.

As the conversation shifted toward how AI could fundamentally reshape Quorum's interface, I worked with the Product Director to reimagine interaction patterns and outline a long-term vision. What started as a focused initiative soon expanded across product and design, guiding how AI could empower government affairs professionals and shape the future of our platform.

The initial challenge: evaluating a beta AI product

As Quorum, an enterprise software for U.S. government affairs, began launching AI features, I analyzed prompts and user adoption across different UI patterns.

Despite common barriers to adopting new technologies, ranging from personal preferences to mistrust. However, embedding small, contextual AI ‚Äúpills‚Äù within the Quorum interface proved highly valuable and became a key driver of adoption.

With a complete overhaul of an existing features leveraging AI, some important questions arose towards this change of paradigm

"As our AI explorations evolved, The challenge wasn't just about adding 'intelligent' features, it was about fundamentally rethinking how we approach product design in an AI-augmented world."

I analyzed prompts and AI capabilities to identify real opportunities where AI could reduce friction and improve workflows, rather than simply adding "intelligent" features.

Analysis Framework

  • Prompt Analysis: Studied how users naturally express their needs and goals
  • Capability Mapping: Identified where AI could genuinely enhance user workflows
  • Friction Reduction: Focused on removing barriers rather than adding complexity
  • Workflow Integration: Designed AI to work within existing processes, not replace them

Collective Learning

I guided my team through benchmarking and hands-on experimentation with AI tools, helping them understand how patterns like copilots and natural language interactions reshape interfaces and processes.

Learning Approach

  • Benchmarking: Studied successful AI implementations across different industries
  • Hands-on Experimentation: Team members directly experienced AI tools to understand capabilities
  • Pattern Recognition: Identified common AI interaction patterns and their implications
  • Process Reimagining: Explored how natural language could reshape traditional interfaces

Vision and Documentation

Since the product team struggled to imagine what an AI-powered interface could look like, I created a vision prototype and documented key AI design patterns. This served as a shared reference for product, engineering, and leadership.

Something very enriching was that I was able to gradually involve the all the designers from my team, and our final delivery, a product overhaul prototype was a shared effort with a lot of different visions and perspectives coming together.

Documentation Deliverables

  • Vision Prototype: Interactive mockup showing AI-augmented workflows
  • Design Patterns: Documented common AI interaction patterns and best practices
  • User Journey Maps: Visualized how AI could enhance specific user workflows

Board Presentation

To bring alignment, I led my team in designing a unified prototype that broke the problem into clear, tangible parts. With this vision, we were able to show executives the many possibilities of how AI is shaping new usability patterns and the opportunities this opens for the product. The prototype became a catalyst for strategic discussions on AI's role in the overall product direction.

"The prototype didn't just show what AI could do‚Äîit demonstrated how AI could serve our users' needs in ways that felt natural and valuable."

The solutions

  • Problem Decomposition: Broke complex AI integration into manageable, understandable pieces
  • Tangible Examples: Used concrete scenarios to illustrate AI's value proposition
  • Strategic Alignment: Connected AI capabilities to business goals and user needs
  • Implementation Roadmap: Provided clear next steps for moving forward

Reflection & Learnings

This project taught me that successful AI integration isn't about the technology‚Äîit's about understanding how AI can serve human needs in ways that feel natural and valuable. The key was shifting from a feature mindset to a user-centered approach.

Key Learnings

  • User-Centered AI: AI should serve user needs, not just showcase technical capabilities
  • Collective Understanding: Teams need hands-on experience with AI to design effectively
  • Vision Prototypes: Tangible examples are crucial for aligning stakeholders around AI strategy
  • Pattern Documentation: Shared design patterns help teams build consistent AI experiences

Thank you for reading this case study. This project fundamentally changed how we approach AI design at Quorum by shifting from a feature-driven to a user-centered methodology.
