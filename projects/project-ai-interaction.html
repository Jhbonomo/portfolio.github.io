How AI Changes the Way We Interact with Products

As Quorum, an enterprise software for US government affairs, started experimenting with AI, our product team faced a key challenge: how to move beyond a feature-driven platform while unlocking AI's potential in ways that truly served user needs.
2023 6 months Design Manager (Quorum)

My Role

When AI became a strategic pillar at Quorum, a US based company for U.S. government affairs, I worked alongside the Product Manager from AI team and the Product designer from this team to help on shaping the vision. I led early research, analyzing prompts and UX patterns to understand how users perceived AI, then helped translate those insights into our first release.

As the conversation shifted toward AI's broader potential, I facilitated design discussions with the Product Director to reimagine interaction patterns and define a long-term vision. What began as a focused experiment soon expanded into a cross-product strategy, shaping how AI empowers government affairs professionals and the future of our platform.

The initial challenge: evaluating a beta AI product

As Quorum began launching AI features, I analyzed prompts and user adoption across different UI patterns. The initial beta release showed promising engagement with 67% of users trying the AI features within the first week, but completion rates dropped to 23% after the first interaction.

I analyzed prompts and AI capabilities to identify real opportunities where AI could reduce friction and improve workflows, rather than simply adding "intelligent" features. Through user interviews and usage analytics, I discovered that users were most frustrated with repetitive data entry tasks and complex filtering operations.

Collective Learning

As Product Design Manager, I promoted initiatives within my team through benchmarking and hands-on experimentation with AI tools to improve our operations. I involved everyone in the transformation, helping them understand how patterns like copilots and natural language interactions are reshaping interfaces and processes. This collaborative approach led to 15 new AI-enhanced feature proposals from the design team.

Learning Approach

  • Benchmarking: Studied successful AI implementations across different industries, analyzing 12+ products including GitHub Copilot, Notion AI, and Microsoft 365 Copilot
  • Hands-on Experimentation: Team members directly experienced AI tools to understand capabilities, with each designer spending 2-3 hours weekly testing new AI features
  • Pattern Recognition: Identified common AI interaction patterns and their implications, documenting 8 key patterns for enterprise software
  • Process Reimagining: Explored how natural language could reshape traditional interfaces, reducing task completion time by 40% in prototype testing

Vision and Documentation

Challenges of New Technology Adoption

  • Every new technology faces barriers to adoption, from personal preferences to broader issues of trust.
  • Redesigning existing features with AI raised critical questions about this paradigm shift and its impact on our overall solutions.
  • Introducing small, contextual AI "pills" within the Quorum interface proved highly valuable for users and became a key driver of adoption.
"As our AI explorations evolved, The challenge wasn't just about adding 'intelligent' features, it was about fundamentally rethinking how we approach product design in an AI-augmented world."

Since the product team struggled to imagine what an AI-powered interface could look like, I created a vision prototype and documented key AI design patterns. This served as a shared reference for product, engineering, and leadership.

Something very enriching was that I was able to gradually involve the all the designers from my team, and our final delivery, a product overhaul prototype was a shared effort with a lot of different visions and perspectives coming together.

  • Capability Mapping: Identified where AI could genuinely enhance user workflows
  • Friction Reduction: Focused on removing barriers rather than adding complexity
  • Workflow Integration: Designed AI to work within existing processes, not replace them

Documentation Deliverables

  • Vision Prototype: Interactive mockup showing AI-augmented workflows
  • Design Patterns: Documented common AI interaction patterns and best practices
  • User Journey Maps: Visualized how AI could enhance specific user workflows
