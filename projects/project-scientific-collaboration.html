Fostering collaboration across Brazil's research ecosystem

The launch of PNIPE, a Ministry of Science and Technology platform that centralizes lab and equipment data, enabling researchers to access resources, collaborate, and strengthen national research infrastructure and public policies.
2020/2021 Product Squad RNP/MCTI

Project Overview & My Role

Led the MVP design of PNIPE, driving collaborative prototyping, stakeholder alignment, and the creation of a design system that balanced usability with government requirements. Transitioned the team to Figma for real-time collaboration and facilitated engagement between users, officials, and engineers.

Discovery & user research

During discovery, we ran focus groups with researchers, lab managers, and policymakers to uncover collaboration pain points, align business goals with user needs, and involve developers early for smoother implementation.

Collaborative design process

Using interactive Figma prototypes, we tested ideas quickly, validating workflows and demonstrating how new features could impact research collaboration and policy-making strategies.

Solution design

Based on validated insights, We designed the MVP to support three major user scenarios: researchers searching for equipment, streamlined resource requesting between institutions, and lab managers updating availability in real-time.

Platform Features

The platform makes Brazil's research infrastructure transparent and accessible, connecting universities and research centers while giving the Ministry a powerful tool to guide policy and foster innovation nationwide.

Government design aystem

A flexible design system adapted from government guidelines ensured consistency across the platform and future iterations.

Implementation & evolution

Post-MVP, we analyzed user behavior via Google Analytics and support feedback to guide a roadmap: improving registration flows, streamlining institution registration, and one initiative during pandemic to create a dashboard to track equipment, to provide information to the Health Ministry during Covid-19 vaccine distribution.

Impact & Outcomes

This project demonstrated how design can enable collaboration on a national scale. By making research infrastructure transparent and accessible, the platform connected universities and research centers while giving the Ministry a powerful tool to inform policy and drive innovation across Brazil.

  • Research Connectivity: Connected universities and research centers across Brazil
  • Policy Intelligence: Provided Ministry with data to shape science policy and infrastructure investments
  • Resource Optimization: Enabled better utilization of existing research infrastructure
  • Innovation Acceleration: Facilitated collaboration that drives scientific advancement
