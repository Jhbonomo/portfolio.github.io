Fostering collaboration across Brazil's research ecosystem

PNIPE is a public platform by the Ministry of Science and Technology that centralizes lab and equipment information, enabling researchers to access resources, collaborate, and inform policies for stronger research infrastructure.
2020/2021 Product Squad RNP/MCTI

Project Overview & My Role

I led the design process for the MVP launch of PNIPE, focusing on collaborative prototyping and stakeholder alignment. My key contributions included transitioning the team to Figma for real-time collaboration, facilitating stakeholder engagement between users, Ministry officials, and engineers, and delivering a comprehensive design system that balanced usability with government requirements.

Discovery & User Research

The discovery phase involved focus groups with researchers, lab managers, and policymakers. Through these sessions, we identified pain points in research infrastructure collaboration, mapped business goals against user needs, and involved developers early to ensure smoother implementation.

User Journey Mapping

We mapped the system information architecture and user journeys to understand pain points and opportunities for collaboration in research infrastructure.

Collaborative Design Process

Using interactive Figma prototypes, we tested ideas quickly, validating workflows and demonstrating how new features could impact research collaboration and policy-making strategies.

Solution Design

Based on validated insights, We designed the MVP to support three major user scenarios: researchers searching for equipment, streamlined resource requesting between institutions, and lab managers updating availability in real-time.

Platform Features

The platform makes Brazil's research infrastructure transparent and accessible, connecting universities and research centers while giving the Ministry a powerful tool to guide policy and foster innovation nationwide.

Design System

A flexible design system adapted from government guidelines ensured consistency across the platform and future iterations.

Implementation & Evolution

After the MVP launch, we analyzed user behavior through Google Analytics and support feedback. These insights guided a new roadmap: improving registration flows, streamlining collaboration between institutions, and creating dashboards to track equipment availability, features with the goal to support the Health Ministry during Covid-19 vaccine distribution.

Impact & Outcomes

This project demonstrated how design can enable collaboration on a national scale. By making research infrastructure transparent and accessible, the platform connected universities and research centers while giving the Ministry a powerful tool to inform policy and drive innovation across Brazil.

  • Research Connectivity: Connected universities and research centers across Brazil
  • Policy Intelligence: Provided Ministry with data to shape science policy and infrastructure investments
  • Resource Optimization: Enabled better utilization of existing research infrastructure
  • Innovation Acceleration: Facilitated collaboration that drives scientific advancement

Key Learnings

This project revealed fundamental insights about designing for complex stakeholder ecosystems and national-scale collaboration.

Critical Discoveries

  • Stakeholder Complexity: Government platforms require balancing multiple stakeholder needs and priorities
  • Collaborative Design: Early developer involvement and Figma collaboration significantly improved outcomes
  • National Scale: Design decisions impact research collaboration across an entire country
  • Policy Integration: Platform design can directly influence government policy and infrastructure decisions

Lasting Influence

  • Figma became the company standard for prototyping in future projects
  • The collaborative design process established new workflows for stakeholder engagement
  • The platform continues to evolve based on user feedback and policy needs
  • The methodology has been applied to other government and research initiatives
